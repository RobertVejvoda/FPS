## Andrea - Employee with car

Andrea is a typical employee who needs to be in the office at least twice a week. Usually he takes a public transport, but sometimes he'd like to use his car. Like his colleagues, he faces issues with parking. He is frustrated with the current system, which requires him to send multiple emails to secure a parking spot. Andrea's experience has been very negative. He desires a fair system where parking slots are distributed evenly, rather than on a "first come, first served" basis. He believes that employees who occasionally need to park in the company's garage should have priority over those who park daily.

### Andrea needs...

Andrea wants a more efficient and transparent parking system. He desires an automated process that eliminates the need for multiple emails and ensures fair distribution of parking slots. Andrea believes that a system prioritizing occasional parkers over daily users would be more equitable. He also wants better communication and real-time updates on parking availability to reduce frustration and improve his overall experience.

## Bob - Employee with motorbike

Bob is similar to Andrea, but he sometimes uses his motorcycle. He'd like to have a dedicated parking slot for motorbikes. Bob finds it inconvenient to park his motorcycle in car spaces, as it often leads to inefficient use of space and potential damage to his bike. He also faces the same frustration with the current parking system, which involves sending multiple emails to secure a spot. Bob desires a more streamlined and fair system that includes dedicated motorcycle parking and prioritizes occasional parkers over daily users.

### Bob needs...

Bob wants a dedicated and secure parking area for motorcycles to prevent damage and optimize space usage. He desires an automated system that simplifies the booking process, reduces the need for multiple emails, and ensures fair distribution of parking slots. Bob also wants real-time updates on parking availability and a system that prioritizes occasional parkers over daily users, similar to Andrea's needs.

## Cecil - Sales with company car

Cecil is a sales representative with a company car and a dedicated parking slot. He appreciates the convenience of having a reserved spot, but he understands the importance of sharing resources. Cecil is required to inform Elvis from HR a day in advance if he plans to use his parking slot. This allows Elvis to offer the slot to other employees when Cecil does not need it. Cecil's proactive communication helps ensure that parking resources are utilized efficiently and fairly. He desires a system that makes it easy to notify HR and allows for better management of parking slots to accommodate everyone's needs.

### Cecil needs...

Cecil wants an easy-to-use system that allows him to quickly notify HR when he does not need his parking slot. He desires a more efficient way to manage his parking reservations, ensuring that his spot can be reallocated to other employees when not in use. Cecil also wants better communication tools to facilitate this process and ensure that parking resources are shared fairly and effectively. An automated system that integrates with his calendar and provides reminders would greatly enhance his experience and contribute to a more organized parking management system.

## David - Employee cheater

David is resourceful and understands that writing emails to secure a parking spot is often ineffective. Instead, she prefers to ask a colleague with a company car and a dedicated parking slot if she can use their spot when they are not using it. If the colleague agrees, David uses the slot without notifying anyone else. She does not see this as unfair to others. Occasionally, David will use a parking slot without prior agreement, which prevents the person who officially booked it from using it. David's approach highlights the inefficiencies and lack of transparency in the current parking system. She desires a more organized and transparent system that ensures fair access to parking for all employees.

### David needs...

David wants a more transparent and organized parking system that eliminates the need for informal agreements and ensures fair access for all employees. She desires an automated process that tracks and manages parking slot usage efficiently, reducing the need for personal arrangements. David also wants real-time updates on parking availability and a system that prioritizes fairness and transparency, ensuring that everyone has an equal opportunity to secure a parking spot.

## Elvis - HR parking responsible

Elvis is responsible for managing parking slot assignments at the company. She receives numerous requests via email and tracks them using an Excel spreadsheet. This manual process is time-consuming and prone to errors. Elvis often struggles to keep up with the volume of requests and ensure that slots are assigned fairly. Requests are handled in the order they arrive, which sometimes leads to dissatisfaction among employees. She finds it challenging to balance the needs of daily parkers with those who only occasionally require a spot. Elvis desires an automated system that can streamline the request process, reduce her workload, and ensure a fair distribution of parking slots. Such a system would allow her to focus on other important tasks and improve overall efficiency.

### Elvis needs...

Elvis wants an automated system that simplifies the parking slot request process and reduces her administrative burden. She desires a solution that can handle requests efficiently, track usage, and ensure fair distribution of slots. Elvis also wants real-time updates and notifications to manage parking assignments better. An integrated system that provides analytics and reporting features would help her make data-driven decisions and improve overall parking management.


## Fiona - HR manager, reports to CEO

Fiona is the HR Manager who oversees the overall parking management system. She values having detailed reports on parking slot usage to make informed decisions. Fiona uses these reports to identify patterns, such as peak usage times and frequently unused slots. This data helps her optimize the allocation of parking resources and address any issues proactively. Fiona desires a system that provides comprehensive and easy-to-understand reports, enabling her to ensure fair access to parking and improve employee satisfaction. An automated reporting feature would significantly enhance her ability to manage parking efficiently and transparently.

### Fiona needs...

Fiona wants a comprehensive reporting system that provides detailed insights into parking slot usage. She desires automated reports that highlight patterns, peak usage times, and underutilized slots. Fiona also wants the ability to generate custom reports to address specific queries and make data-driven decisions. An integrated system that offers real-time analytics and visualizations would help her optimize parking resource allocation and improve overall employee satisfaction. Additionally, Fiona seeks a solution that ensures transparency and fairness in parking management, aligning with the company's goals and policies.

## Godric - CEO

Godric is the CEO who prioritizes sustainability and environmental friendliness in all aspects of the company's operations. He is committed to reducing the company's carbon footprint and encourages employees to use public transportation, carpool, or cycle to work. Godric supports the implementation of green initiatives, such as installing electric vehicle charging stations and promoting the use of hybrid or electric cars. He believes in a fair and equitable parking system that incentivizes eco-friendly commuting options. Godric desires a parking management system that not only ensures fair access to parking slots but also aligns with the company's sustainability goals by rewarding employees who choose environmentally friendly transportation methods.

### Godric needs...

Godric wants a parking management system that supports the company's sustainability goals. He desires features that incentivize eco-friendly commuting options, such as priority parking for carpoolers and electric vehicle charging stations. Godric also wants the system to provide data on the environmental impact of commuting choices, helping to track and reduce the company's carbon footprint. Additionally, he seeks a fair and transparent system that ensures equitable access to parking slots while promoting green initiatives and rewarding employees who choose sustainable transportation methods.
